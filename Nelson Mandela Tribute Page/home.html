Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela

A South African anti-apartheid activist and politician who served as the first president of South Africa from 1994 to 1999

Nelson Mandela (left) holding hands with his wife Winnie Mandela (right)

Here's a timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 18 July 1918 - Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela is born in Mvezo, a village near Mthatha in the Transkei region of South Africa.
  • 1930 - His father dies, and he is taken to be raised by the Acting King of the Thembu people, Chief Jongintaba Dalindyebo.
  • 1934 - Undergoes initiation; enrolls at Clarkebury Boarding Institute at Engcobo.
  • 1937 - Enrolls at Healdtown, the Wesleyan College at Fort Beaufort.
  • 1939 - Enrolls at the University of Fort Hare in Alice.
  • 1940 - Is expelled from the University of Fort Hare for participating in a student protest.
  • 1941 - Runs away to Johannesburg to avoid an arranged marriage.
  • 1942 - Completes his BA through the University of South Africa (Unisa).
  • 1943 - Enrolls for an LLB at the University of the Witwatersrand (Wits University).
  • 1944 - Co-founds the African National Congress (ANC) Youth League; marries Evelyn Mase.
  • 1948 - Is elected National Secretary of the ANC Youth League.
  • 1951 - Is elected President of the ANC Youth League.
  • 1952 - Volunteers in the Defiance Campaign against apartheid laws; sentenced to nine months in prison.
  • 1956 - Arrested and charged with high treason, along with 155 other activists.
  • 1958 - Divorces Evelyn Mase and marries Winnie Madikizela.
  • 1961 - Acquitted in the Treason Trial; goes underground to form an armed wing of the ANC.
  • 1962 - Secretly leaves the country for military training; arrested and sentenced to five years in prison.
  • 1964 - Sentenced to life imprisonment in the Rivonia Trial.
  • 1990 - Released from prison after 27 years; leads ANC delegation in talks with the government to end apartheid.
  • 1994 - Votes in South Africa's first democratic election; inaugurated as President.
  • 1999 - Steps down as president after one term; starts the Nelson Mandela Foundation.
  • 2004 - Retires from public life.
  • 2008 - Encourages youth to create a better world, prompting the establishment of Mandela Day.
  • 2009 - Nelson Mandela International Day is proclaimed by the United Nations.
  • 2010 - Publishes "Conversations with Myself," a collection of personal writings.
  • 2011 - Publishes "Nelson Mandela By Himself: The Authorized Book of Quotations."
  • 2013 - Passes away peacefully at his home in Johannesburg at the age of 95.
